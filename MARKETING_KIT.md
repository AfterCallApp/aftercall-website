## 1. The thing we are selling

AfterCall is a mobile app, iOS at launch, built for EMS providers —
EMTs, Paramedics, and EMS students.

It does six things:

1. **Logs every call** in an 8-step guided flow (chief complaint,
   acuity, vitals, interventions, GCS, outcome).
2. **Tracks shifts** — acuity mix, busy days, recent calls, clinical
   load.
3. **Holds the wellness work** — guided reflections after tough calls,
   end-of-shift debriefs, daily mood / stress / energy / sleep
   check-ins.
4. **Connects the crew** — station orgs, peer support chats, direct
   messaging, a community feed.
5. **Surfaces what's changing in EMS** — a curated trending feed of
   protocol updates, news, and deep-dives.
6. **Tracks clinical edge** — every IV/IO, airway, medication, and
   cardioversion logged into a skill momentum view month over month.

Plus **AfterCall AI**: writes the call narrative from your 8-step entry
and powers the end-of-shift debrief by reading across your calls + your
journal.

**Pricing.** Free tier with unlimited call logging, journaling,
community, and 30-day history. Pro at **$59.99/year (~$5/mo)** or
**$7.99/mo**, with a **7-day free trial**. Pro adds AI narrative + AI
debrief, full 12-month analytics, PDF export, AI wellness chat,
trending deep-dives, and advanced skill momentum.

**Launches** EMS Week 2026 — **May 17, 2026** — iOS only at launch.

**Built by** Jordan Appelbaum, Founder & CEO, AfterCall LLC.
Independent. No corporate backing.

## 2. The person we are selling to

A working EMT, Paramedic, or EMS student. They:

- Run real calls. Pediatric arrests. Overdoses. MVCs. Refusals at 3am.
- Don't get debriefed unless they make it happen themselves.
- Document into systems built for billing and compliance, not for them.
- Drive back to quarters in silence sometimes.
- Speak their own language: "cleared", "RMA", "trauma alert",
  "narc count", "shift-from-hell", "we got slammed today".
- Know — and quote — the burnout numbers but rarely act on them
  because nothing in their tooling treats them like a clinician.
- Are not impressed by SaaS marketing. Are very impressed by tools
  that obviously came from someone who has actually run the call.

**They are not the customer of EMS software today.** Their agency is.
That's the wedge.

### 3.1 The promise

Concise: **Your calls, captured.**

Long form: AfterCall is the app for the moment after the call. Built
by EMS providers, for EMS providers.

### 3.2 The guarantee in one sentence

> The first app that treats EMS providers like clinicians, not data
> entry clerks.

### 3.3 The five things every piece of marketing must feel

1. **Earned.** Built by someone who has actually been on the call.
2. **Quiet confidence.** Doesn't sell hard. Doesn't oversell. Just
   shows you the tool and gets out of the way.
3. **Premium clinical.** Apple Health / Whoop / Linear / Robinhood —
   not "wellness app pastel."
4. **A little sad-but-strong.** Acknowledges the weight without
   wallowing in it. Never pity. Never therapy-speak.
5. **In-language.** EMS providers should read it and recognize their
   own internal monologue.

### 3.4 What we will never sound like

- Corporate SaaS ("transform your workflow", "supercharge", "leverage")
- BetterHelp / Headspace ("you matter", "your mental health journey")
- Cop or military bro-speak ("warriors", "thin line", "unsung heroes")
- Hospital marketing ("compassionate care", "comprehensive solution")
- AI-hype ("revolutionary AI-powered intelligent assistant")

If a draft sounds like any of those, kill it.

## 4. Voice & tone

### 4.1 House voice

- **Short sentences.** Periods, not commas.
- **In-the-room, not in-the-boardroom.** "After the call." not "post-
  incident."
- **Specific clinical nouns.** TXA. Push-dose epi. EtCO₂. Cardioversion.
  GCS. Vague verbs are out; verbs from the unit are in.
- **One feeling per sentence.** Don't pile up adjectives.
- **No exclamation marks.** Ever. The voice never raises its volume.
- **The reader is competent.** Don't explain EMS to EMS. Explain
  AfterCall to EMS.

### 4.2 Voice samples lifted from the actual product

These are real prompts the app already uses with providers. They are
the exact register marketing should match:

> What was the hardest minute of this call, and what was happening?

> What is your body telling you right now after that call?

> What detail keeps replaying most right now?

> Anything from today you haven't actually processed yet?

> What helps you transition from unit mode to home mode tonight?

> How is your body at clock-out?
> _quick options:_ Beat up · Fatigued but steady · Adrenalized ·
> Surprisingly okay

> How sharp did you feel overall this shift?
> _quick options:_ Locked in · Mostly solid · Inconsistent ·
> Running behind all day

> What are you proud of from this shift that no one else may have
> noticed?

If a marketing line could plausibly be a question in the app's
guided reflection, you are on voice. If it could not, redraft.

### 4.3 Vocabulary cheat sheet

Use:
- after the call · between calls · clear · clearing · post-shift ·
  on shift · on the unit · in quarters · clock-out · running · slammed
- log · capture · debrief · check-in · reflect · process · carry
- crew · partner · station · org · provider · clinician
- skill · intervention · acuity · narrative · GCS · vitals · outcome
- AfterCall AI ✦  (always with the four-point star and the AI purple)

Avoid:
- workflow · solutions · empower · intelligent · seamless · holistic ·
  next-generation · revolutionary · ecosystem · synergy · journey ·
  warrior · hero · stress-buster · self-care

### 4.4 Three voice targets, picked deliberately

- **The provider** (default). Direct, clinical, dry, occasionally dark.
- **The founder, Jordan.** First-person, plain, sometimes confessional.
  Used in long-form posts, founder-led video, LinkedIn announcements.
  Pulls verbatim from his on-site quote when possible.
- **The product** (rare). Used inside the app and in feature
  descriptions; tight, helpful, almost a bullet. Never used for
  emotional copy.

### 9.2 Typography

- Display + UI typeface: **Inter**, weights 800–900 for headlines,
  500–700 for UI, 400 for body.
- Inside the app the splash uses SF Pro Display. For static marketing,
  Inter is the canonical face. Don't introduce a third typeface.
- Letter-spacing on headlines is **tight** — around -1.5 to -2px on
  big sizes. Without it, the brand goes generic immediately.
- All-caps is rare. Reserved for the footer cap and section labels.

### 9.3 The motifs we own

Three repeating visual elements — every design should use at least one,
and the kit should not invent new motifs:

1. **The EKG sweep line.** A clean, single-stroke heartbeat trace, drawn
   in primary blue at low opacity behind hero content, or as a colored
   underline beneath a number. Always one continuous path. Animated on
   the website; static in print.

2. **The phone frame.** Real iPhone screenshots from the repo, dropped
   into a clean dark bezel (#1c1c1e) with rounded corners (radius 32–40
   depending on size). Light shadow. Never a generic stock phone mockup.

3. **The eyebrow chip.** A small pill (`background:#EEF4FD` /
   `color:#1F5FA3`) with a subtle pulsing dot that says one of:
   _Launching EMS Week 2026_ · _For EMS providers_ · _Built by EMS_.
   Lives above headlines.

### 9.4 What we will not put in a design

- Generic medical clip-art (red cross, stethoscope, ambulance silhouette)
- Stock-photo paramedics
- Drop-shadows under phones in dark mode
- Gradient text
- Emojis as the primary visual element
- More than three weights of Inter on one design
- Centered-everything layouts (we left-align headlines)
- Pastels of any kind
- The American flag

both the Canva (Claude Design) MCP and the GitHub MCP, signed in with
an `@aftercallapp.com` email so the brand-protection check passes.

It assumes this `MARKETING_KIT.md` file lives at the root of
`aftercallapp/aftercall-website` on `main`.

```text
You are producing the AfterCall EMS Week 2026 launch marketing kit.
Use the Canva tools (Claude Design) for every design and the GitHub
MCP for source material. Do not invent voice, do not invent stats,
do not generate fake screenshots.

STEP 1 — Read the kit:
Pull `MARKETING_KIT.md` from the `main` branch of
`aftercallapp/aftercall-website`. It is the source of truth for
voice, color, type, claims, and design specs. If anything in this
prompt and the kit conflict, the kit wins.

hex values match Section 9.1 of the kit and use them anywhere
```
